OUR MISSION

The Grenada National Museum (GNM) will collect, preserve, research, interpret, display and promote the historical, natural and cultural heritage of Grenada, Carriacou and Petite Martinique through its exhibits, educational programs, and cultural and historical experiences that will foster an understanding and interest in the heritage of the people of Grenada. The Grenada National Museum shall be the premier resource for residents, visitors, students and scholars who are interested in learning about the historical, natural and cultural heritage of Grenada, Carriacou and Petite Martinique

OUR NEW DIRECTION

The GNM, though in existence for the past 47 years, remains in a very basic state because of limited resources, particularly financial and technical. Its greatest ac hievement has been its survival in the face of tremendous challenges over the years. As a means to remedying its present situation, the GNM endeavors to become a state of the art museum that is accessible and inclusive of Grenada's diverse history and cult ure as it prepares for its 50th anniversary in April 2026, embodying its goal of being the foremost institution for the preservation and interpretation

of the islands' cultural heritage. In a concerted effort to enhance the GNM, the Board of Directors and staff have embarked on a journey that will see the GNM on the road to becoming the premier institution in Grenada for the preservation and interpretation of the islands' historical and cultural heritage. Please join us on this most exciting journey as we s hare the stories of who we are and the beauty of our tri - island state.

ABOUT US

The Grenada National Museum celebrates the unique heritage and culture of the people of Grenada, Carriacou and Petite Martinique. It is dedicated to the preservation, interpretation and promotion of the islands' history and culture. Come explore centuries of history, culture and art in one of the islands' oldest buildings in downtown, historical St. George's. The Museum is the central attraction of an educational and visitor complex that will be an inviting cultural space in the heart of St. George's, affording a reprieve from its usual hustle and bustle. We also invite you to join us for lectures, art exhibitions, and other special events throughout the year.

HISTORY

The Grenada National Museum at the corner of Young and Monckton Streets, St. George's opened its doors to the public on 17 April 1976. Under the instigation of then Prime Minister Sir Eric M. Gairy, it was founded by a group of expatriates and citizens who came t ogether to promote the awareness of history, culture and heritage by displaying elements of Grenadian history and culture. For many years thereafter it was the sole functioning museum in the country, and has remained

the only one with a national agenda. The GNM's collection of artifacts consist of objects illustrating Grenada's history and culture, including an assortment of pre - colonial Indigenous (Amerindian) artifacts. Displayed also are agricultural machinery once used in the manufacture of sugar and rum, whaling equipment from the whaling station at Glover Island, the supposed bathtub of the French Empress Josephine, historical pictures and documents, copies of historical maps, and stories of Grenada's almost two millennia history and culture.

OUR DIRECTOR

John Angus Martin is a historian, archivist and museum curator specializing in Grenada's history and culture. He holds a BS in Biological Sciences, MA in History, and MS in Agricultural and Applied Economics. He is currently completing a PhD in Herit age Management in the Faculty of Archaeology, Leiden University. He spent three years as a Peace Corps volunteer in Sierra Leone as a teacher and agricultural extension agent. He has worked as the Director of the Saba Archaeological Center, Saba, Reference Archivist at the Cushing Memorial Library, Texas A&M University, Director/Curator of the Grenada National Museum, Country Desk Officer at the US Peace Corps, part - time lecturer in the School of Arts and Sciences, St George's University, and member of the Grenada National Reparations Committee. Martin is the author/co - author/editor of several books and articles, including: A to Z of Grenada Heritage (2022) and Island Caribs and French Settlers in Grenada, 1498 - 1763 (2013)
